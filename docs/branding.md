# BeanBook — Brand

The promise, the voice, and what we say no to.

## What BeanBook is

A quiet logbook for home coffee. You log a bag, you log a shot, you can repeat what worked. That's the product.

The user is a person who pulls one or two shots in the morning, mostly the same coffee for a week or two at a time, and occasionally dials in something new. They are not a "creator." They are not chasing streaks. They want their phone to get out of the way.

## What BeanBook is not

This is the load-bearing part of the brand. Whenever a feature decision is unclear, these say no for us:

- **Not a social app.** No followers, no feeds, no comments, no shares-as-product-loop. You can export your data, that's the extent of "share."
- **Not a streak app.** No daily goals, no notifications nagging you, no "you missed a day."
- **Not a scoring algorithm.** No ranking your shots against other people's. No "your coffee score." Ratings are personal, 1–5 stars, end of story.
- **Not a subscription.** Pro is a one-time purchase. Owning the app means owning the app.
- **Not a journal/blog hybrid.** Notes are a single field, not a rich-text editor with images and tags. If you want a journal, use a journal app.

## Voice

**Restrained, editorial, second-person, plainspoken.**

- Sentence-case, never title-case in body copy. Headings can be sentence-case with serif type doing the work.
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Use the word the user would use. "Shot," "pull," "dial in," "bag," "yield," "dose," "grind." Never "session," "entry," "experience," "journey."
- Never exclaim. No "!" in shipped copy. No "Yay" or "Nice work" celebrations.
- Don't praise the user for using the app. Logging a brew is not an achievement.
- When something is optional, say "optional." When something is permanent, say "you can change this anytime" only if it's true.

### Touchstones

- "A quiet logbook." (the one-liner)
- "For the coffee you brew at home." (the audience line)
- "No streaks. No social. No scoring algorithm." (the promise)
- "Pull the shot." (a step header)
- "How was it?" (a step header)
- "Skip — no bag." (an opt-out)

### Things to avoid

- "Welcome aboard!" / "Let's get started!" — too eager.
- "Pro tip:" / "Did you know?" — condescending.
- "We've got you covered." — corporate.
- "Sip happy." / "Brewtiful." — pun-coffee voice. We are not a 2014 startup blog.
- Stat callouts during onboarding ("Join 10,000 coffee nerds!") — there isn't a number we'd ever quote.

## Pro positioning

Pro is a one-time purchase. This is the single most important commercial fact about the app and the message users should encounter wherever Pro is mentioned.

The three-line value prop, in priority order:

1. **One-time purchase.** No subscription, ever.
2. **Unlimited everything.** Bags, brews, recipes — quotas lifted.
3. **Future Pro features included.** Stats, export, themes — included when they ship.

Wherever you mention Pro:

- Lead with "one-time" or "one purchase," not the price.
- Always pair the price with the word "once" — `Unlock Pro · $X once`, not `Unlock for $X`.
- Mention Family Sharing — it's a real feature, it differentiates from subscription apps.

Do **not**:

- Use scarcity copy ("Limited time!" — there's no urgency).
- Use comparison copy ("Other apps charge $X/year" — it punches down).
- Bundle Pro with a free-trial flow. There's no trial. There's a generous free tier and a one-time unlock.

## Naming

- **BeanBook** — one word, capital B, capital B. Not "Bean Book," not "Beanbook," not "BB."
- The app is referred to as "BeanBook," not "the app" or "BeanBook app."
- The Pro tier is **BeanBook Pro** in marketing surfaces, **Pro** when context is unambiguous (settings, in-app upsells).
- Methods are spelled how the espresso/specialty community spells them: **Espresso**, **Pour Over** (two words), **AeroPress** (camelCase), **French Press**, **Moka Pot**, **Cold Brew**.

## Color & visual identity

See `design.md` for tokens. The brand-level rules:

- **Manual themes only.** BeanBook does not follow system dark mode. Most palettes keep the editorial paper feel; `Midnight` is the single intentional dark palette for users who want a quieter low-light surface.
- **Forest green is the primary accent.** Cream/off-white is the surface. Black-ink is for type.
- **Serif headlines, system body.** No display-fancy fonts. The serif does the editorial work; the rest gets out of the way.
- **Hairline rules over boxes.** When you need to separate things, use a 0.5pt rule, not a card border.
- **No gradients in CTAs.** The accent pill has a soft glow shadow; that's the only chrome.
- **No emoji in shipped copy.** SF Symbols only, and sparingly.

## Microcopy patterns

| Situation | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Optional field | "(optional)" inline, never "Optional:" prefix |
| Empty state | One sentence, then a single primary CTA. No three-bullet "here's how it works." |
| Error | What happened, then what to do. Two sentences max. |
| Quota wall | Contextual: "You've reached the free limit of N brews." Then offer Pro. Never block the current action mid-flow without an offer. |
| Confirmation | "Saved." (period, not exclamation). Show the relevant value (`1:2.00 · 30s`), then dismiss. |
